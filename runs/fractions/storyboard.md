# Phase 3 — STORYBOARD

## Beat 1 — Two empty glasses appear
**On screen:** Two tall, identical containers (white-outlined rectangles) fade in
side by side, left and right. Both are empty.
**What changes:** The containers appear from nothing.
**Child concludes:** "Two glasses."

## Beat 2 — Fill the left glass almost full
**On screen:** Blue liquid rises inside the left container, stopping high up —
roughly three-quarters of the way.
**What changes:** A continuous blue rectangle grows upward from the bottom of the
left container and stops near the top.
**Child concludes:** "That glass is almost full."

## Beat 3 — Fill the right glass with a tiny splash
**On screen:** Blue liquid rises inside the right container, stopping very low —
just a sliver at the bottom.
**What changes:** A continuous blue rectangle grows upward from the bottom of the
right container and stops barely above the bottom edge.
**Child concludes:** "That glass has just a little bit."

---

*The physical referents now exist. The child sees two amounts — a big one and a
tiny one. No symbols yet.*

---

## Beat 4 — Lay a ruler on the left glass (quarters)
**On screen:** Three yellow tick marks appear on the outside of the left container,
dividing its height into 4 equal parts. The liquid sits exactly at the 3rd tick.
After the ticks are visible, a small label "3/4" fades in beside the container.
**What changes:** Tick marks appear on the container exterior; then the label appears.
**Child concludes:** "The liquid reaches the 3rd line out of 4. That's three-fourths."

## Beat 5 — Lay a ruler on the right glass (eighths)
**On screen:** Seven yellow tick marks appear on the outside of the right container,
dividing its height into 8 equal parts. The liquid sits exactly at the 1st tick.
After the ticks are visible, a small label "1/8" fades in beside the container.
**What changes:** Tick marks appear on the container exterior; then the label appears.
**Child concludes:** "The liquid reaches the 1st line out of 8. That's one-eighth."

---

*Both amounts are named. But their rulers are different — the left glass has
4 marks, the right glass has 8. To pour and read the result, we need the same
ruler.*

---

## Beat 6 — Switch the left glass to the same ruler (quarters → eighths)
**On screen:** The left container's 3 tick marks and "3/4" label fade out. A brief
pause. Then 7 new yellow tick marks fade in on the outside, dividing the same
container into 8 equal parts. The liquid has not moved at all — it still sits at
exactly the same height — but now it aligns with the 6th tick. A new label "6/8"
fades in beside the container.
**What changes:** Only the ruler changes. The liquid stays put. The label updates.
**Child concludes:** "The amount didn't change — we just used more lines, and now
it reaches the 6th mark out of 8. Same glass, same amount, just a finer ruler."

---

*Now both glasses speak the same language — eighths. The left glass is at 6, the
right glass is at 1. We can pour.*

---

## Beat 7 — Pour the right glass into the left glass
**On screen:** The liquid in the right container shrinks downward to nothing
(the container empties). Simultaneously, the liquid in the left container rises
from the 6th tick mark to the 7th tick mark.
**What changes:** Right fill shrinks to zero. Left fill grows by the same small
amount. Total liquid is conserved — nothing created, nothing lost.
**Child concludes:** "I poured the little bit in, and the level went up. It rose
one more mark."

## Beat 8 — See the answer as a visible amount (no number yet)
**On screen:** Hold on the left container, now alone on screen (or the right
container is empty/dimmed). The liquid sits clearly at the 7th tick of 8. No
numeral labels this level yet.
**What changes:** Nothing moves. The child looks.
**Child concludes:** "It's almost full — that makes sense. I started with almost
a full glass and added a little more, so now it's even more full. And it's
definitely more than what I started with, not less."

## Beat 9 — Name the answer
**On screen:** A label "7/8" fades in beside the left container, at the level of
the liquid.
**What changes:** Only the label appears. The amount was already there.
**Child concludes:** "The liquid reaches the 7th line out of 8. The answer is 7/8."

---

## Verification against iron rules

**Rule 1 — Child-first, physical-second, math-last.**
Every beat is described in a child's language: "almost full," "tiny splash,"
"the level went up," "same glass, same amount." No beat is justified by adult
math ("because the denominators must match"). The denominator conversion is felt
as "switching to the same ruler." ✓

**Rule 2 — Only true frames.**
No beat shows an incorrect state. The wrong answer (4/12) never appears. Every
frame shows a physically correct amount. During the pour, the total liquid is
conserved — one shrinks, the other grows by the same amount. ✓

**Rule 3 — No symbol before its referent.**
- Containers appear (beats 1) before any labels.
- Liquid levels appear (beats 2-3) before any labels.
- Tick marks appear before their labels (beats 4-5: ticks first, then "3/4"/"1/8").
- The combined level is visible at the 7th mark (beat 8) before "7/8" appears (beat 9). ✓

## Verification against required sequence

1. **Un-symbol the problem:** Beats 1-5 turn 3/4 and 1/8 into visible liquid
   levels with rulers. ✓
2. **Show the starting state:** Beats 2-3 show amounts the child already accepts
   ("almost full" and "a tiny bit"). ✓
3. **Perform the physical action:** Beats 6-7 (re-ruler, then pour). ✓
4. **Land on visible answer:** Beat 8 — the level at the 7th mark, no numeral. ✓
5. **Overlay measurement/naming:** Beat 9 — "7/8" appears on what's already
   visible. ✓

## Verification against forbidden representation

No beat shows countable cells, separate numerator/denominator piles, or
partitioned bar interiors. The fill is always a single continuous rectangle. Tick
marks are on the exterior (a ruler alongside the amount), not internal grid
lines. ✓
