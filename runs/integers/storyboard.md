# Storyboard

Problem: (+5) + (−3)
Primitive: Position on a line (Walk a distance composition)

---

## Beat 1 — The path

**On screen:** A horizontal number line fades in, spanning −1 to 7, with tick
marks at each integer and small position numbers below each tick.

**Change:** The stage is set — a road with distance markers.

**Child concludes:** "There's a path with marks on it. I know what a path is."

---

## Beat 2 — The walker arrives

**On screen:** A yellow dot fades in at position 0 on the number line.

**Change:** Someone has arrived and is standing at the start of the path.

**Child concludes:** "Someone is standing at zero — the start."

---

## Beat 3 — Walk forward

**On screen:** The dot slides smoothly from 0 to 5. A green arrow appears above
the line from 0 to 5, showing the direction of travel (pointing right).

**Change:** The walker moved forward five steps.

**Child concludes:** "They walked forward! Now they're way over there at 5."

---

## Beat 4 — Label the forward walk

**On screen:** The text "+5" fades in above the green arrow.

**Change:** The forward walk gets a name.

**Child concludes:** "+5 means go forward five steps."

---

## Beat 5 — Walk backward

**On screen:** The dot slides from 5 back to 2. A red arrow appears above the
line from 5 to 2, pointing left (backward). It sits slightly higher than the
green arrow to avoid overlap.

**Change:** The walker turned around and walked backward three steps.

**Child concludes:** "They went backward! They're closer to where they started.
Going backward ate up some of the going forward."

---

## Beat 6 — Label the backward walk

**On screen:** The text "−3" fades in above the red arrow.

**Change:** The backward walk gets a name.

**Child concludes:** "−3 means go backward three steps."

---

## Beat 7 — Where did you end up?

**On screen:** The green and red arrows dim to low opacity. The dot at position 2
pulses (briefly scales up and back down). A white brace appears below the line
spanning from 0 to 2, drawing attention to the gap between start and landing
spot.

**Change:** Attention shifts from the journey to the destination — the landing
spot is the star now.

**Child concludes:** "The answer is where I'm standing. I didn't go all the way
back to zero, but I'm not at 5 anymore either. I'm right here at 2."

---

## Beat 8 — Name the answer

**On screen:** The text "= 2" fades in next to the brace below the line.

**Change:** The landing position gets its number.

**Child concludes:** "The answer is 2. Forward five, backward three, end up at
two. That makes sense — backward undoes some of the forward."

---

## Iron-rule verification

**Rule 1 — Child-first, physical-second, math-last:**
Every beat is described in walking / direction / position language. No beat
justifies itself with "because adding a negative is subtraction." The child
feels forward-then-backward. ✓

**Rule 2 — Only true frames:**
The walker visits exactly three positions: 0, 5, 2. All are correct. No wrong
position, no wrong arrow, no misleading intermediate is ever shown. ✓

**Rule 3 — No symbol before its referent:**
- "+5" (Beat 4) appears after the forward walk is visible (Beat 3). ✓
- "−3" (Beat 6) appears after the backward walk is visible (Beat 5). ✓
- "= 2" (Beat 8) appears after the landing spot is highlighted (Beat 7). ✓

**Forbidden representation check:**
No beat presents two separate groups of countable objects. The animation shows
one traveler on one continuous path. Arrows represent direction of travel, not
piles of things. The wrong act (dump two groups together, count 8) has no
physical affordance here. ✓
